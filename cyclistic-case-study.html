Cyclistic Case Study

Increasing membership numbers

The Scenario

The director of marketing at Cyclistic believes the company’s future success depends on maximizing the number of annual memberships. Therefore, your team wants to understand how casual riders and annual members use Cyclistic bikes differently. From these insights our analyst team will design a new marketing strategy to convert casual riders into annual members. But first, Cyclistic executives must approve our team’s recommendations, so they must be backed up with compelling data insights and professional data visualizations.

The Task

I have been assigned to answer, "How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?" I will be answering this and giving recommendations with the intent of converting casual riders into membership holders.

The Findings

I was able to identify a few key differences between members and casual riders. Members tend to ride more on weekdays and casual riders tend to ride for a longer period of time. Since the membership model is meant to save customers money when riding for a longer period of time, the marketing team should be able to come up with a way to advertise this. This is great because it indicates that instead of having to run a sale for attention, public education should be enough to drive casual users to sign up for memberships!
